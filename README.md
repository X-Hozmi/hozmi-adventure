
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the plains as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his mule onward.  His quest: to reach the fabled city of Zerzura, whispered to hold the secrets of immortality.  Bandits, treacherous mountain passes, and the ever-present threat of starvation were his constant companions.  Each sunrise brought a new challenge, a new test of his resilience, his faith, and the ragged map clutched tight in his hand – a testament to the perilous allure of medieval exploration.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the 'Stardust Wanderer' through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her mission: to locate a rumored Dyson sphere, a megastructure said to hold unimaginable power.  Navigating asteroid fields, dodging pirate vessels from the Andromeda galaxy, and wrestling with the unpredictable quirks of faster-than-light travel were commonplace.  The vast, unforgiving expanse of space was a daunting but intoxicating landscape, offering a future-era adventure as perilous and thrilling as any medieval journey across the unknown.
