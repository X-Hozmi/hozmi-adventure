
The year is 1347.  Sir Reginald, his armor gleaming faintly under the bruised twilight sky, urged his destrier onward.  The rough track, barely a path through the dense Ardennes forest, was a treacherous labyrinth of mud, fallen branches, and the ever-present threat of bandits.  Each creak of his saddle, each rustle in the undergrowth, sharpened his senses.  His quest – to reach the Holy City of Jerusalem and secure a relic rumored to hold unimaginable power – was a perilous journey through hostile lands, a test of endurance against both the elements and the savagery of man.


Alternatively, in the 27th century, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the *Starseeker* through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Faster-than-light travel blurred the cosmos into streaks of incandescent color as they navigated asteroid fields and dodged the gravitational pull of rogue planets.  Their mission: to chart the newly discovered Kepler-186f system, a potential new home for humanity.  The vast, unpredictable expanse of space was a far cry from the familiar Earth, a constant challenge of navigation, resource management, and the ever-present danger of unknown alien encounters.
