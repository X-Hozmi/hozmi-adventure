
The year is 1347.  Dust billowed behind Elara's mule as she crested the ridge, the sun beating down on her worn leather jerkin.  Before her stretched the endless expanse of the Rhône valley, a ribbon of green snaking towards the distant, mist-shrouded Alps.  This was no pilgrimage, no merchant's journey; this was a flight from a shadowed past, a desperate gamble for a new life in a far-off land whispered of in tavern tales – a land of sun-drenched vineyards and olive groves, a world away from the famine and plague that ravaged her homeland.  Each day was a test of endurance, a dance with bandits and the unpredictable whims of nature.


Centuries later, Captain Rexx slammed his fist on the console, the holographic map of the Andromeda galaxy shimmering before him.  His starship, the *Stardust Drifter*, was a speck in a cosmic ocean, hurtling towards the uncharted nebula of Xylos.  The mission: to retrieve a vital energy source rumored to hold the key to interstellar peace.  The risks were immense:  unpredictable wormholes, hostile alien life, and the ever-present threat of pirate raiders. But Rexx, seasoned veteran of a thousand star-jumps, felt the familiar thrill of the unknown, the siren call of adventure echoing through the void.
