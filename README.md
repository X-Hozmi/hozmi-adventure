Whether a merchant leading a caravan of precious goods, a pilgrim seeking holy sites, or a knight answering a distant summons, every journey was a perilous leap into the unknown. Dense, bandit-infested forests, treacherous mountain passes, and vast, desolate plains lay between destinations, while the constant threats of wild animals, disease, and sudden, unforgiving weather made every mile a test of endurance and wit.

Yet, it was this very danger that imbued medieval travel with its profound sense of adventure.
