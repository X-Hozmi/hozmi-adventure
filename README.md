
The year is 1347.  Sir Reginald, his worn leather jerkin clinging to his lean frame, spurred his destrier onward.  The trail, barely more than a rutted track, wound through the dense Ardennes forest, a tapestry of shadow and whispering leaves.  His quest: to reach the legendary city of Ys, rumored to lie hidden beyond the treacherous Black Mire.  Each creak of his saddle, each rustle in the undergrowth, heightened the sense of peril and unknown wonders that lay ahead, a symphony of danger and anticipation echoing the thrum of his own heartbeat.


Centuries hence, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the 'Stardust Drifter' through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her destination: Kepler-186f, a potentially habitable exoplanet light-years from Earth.  The vast, star-strewn canvas of space pressed against the viewport, a breathtaking and terrifying reminder of the scale of her mission.  Navigating asteroid fields and dodging rogue plasma bursts, she relied on her instincts and advanced technology, a far cry from the medieval knight’s reliance on horseflesh and luck, yet the same thrill of exploration pulsed through her veins, the human hunger for the unknown an eternal constant.
