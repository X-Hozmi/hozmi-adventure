
The year is 1347.  Sir Reginald, his battered surcoat flapping in the wind, urged his weary steed onward.  The trail, little more than a rutted track, snaked through the unforgiving Ardennes forest.  Each rustle of leaves, each shadow cast by the ancient oaks, held the potential for danger – bandits, wild beasts, or the ever-present threat of starvation.  His quest, to reach the Holy Land and retrieve a sacred relic, was a perilous journey fraught with hardship, but the glory, he hoped, would be worth the price.  He pressed on, the setting sun painting the sky in hues of blood orange and bruised purple, mirroring the relentless struggle of his adventure.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova gripped the controls of the *Stardust Drifter*, the nebula of Xylos swirling outside her viewport.  Her mission: to locate the lost colony of Kepler-186f, a journey spanning light years and fraught with dangers far beyond medieval bandits.  Navigating treacherous asteroid fields and dodging pirate ships from the Andromeda galaxy demanded precision and courage.  The vast emptiness of space, both beautiful and terrifying, pressed in on her, a stark contrast to the claustrophobic confines of her ship. Yet, the allure of discovery, the hope of finding the lost colonists, spurred her onward.
