
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his mule onward.  He clutched the worn map, its parchment brittle with age, depicting the rumored location of El Dorado, a city of gold whispered about in hushed tones in taverns across Europe.  His journey was fraught with peril – bandits lurked in the shadows of ancient forests, treacherous mountain passes tested his endurance, and the ever-present threat of disease hung heavy in the air.  Each sunrise brought the hope of discovery, each sunset the gnawing fear of failure.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the 'Stardust Drifter' through the swirling nebula of Xylos, its multi-hued gases painting a breathtaking, yet treacherous, backdrop to their journey.  Her mission: to locate the lost colony of Kepler-186f, a planet rumored to hold the key to interstellar faster-than-light travel.  Navigating asteroid fields, evading pirate vessels, and battling the unpredictable gravitational pulls of newly discovered celestial bodies tested the limits of her ship and her crew.  The vastness of space offered unimaginable beauty, but also an isolating, ever-present danger.
