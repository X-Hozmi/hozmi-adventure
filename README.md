
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his mule onward.  The journey from Canterbury to Rome was a perilous one, fraught with bandits lurking in shadowed forests, treacherous mountain passes, and the ever-present threat of disease.  Each sunrise brought new challenges – navigating unfamiliar terrain, bartering for meager sustenance in hostile villages, and constantly scanning the horizon for signs of danger. Yet, the promise of the Holy City, of absolution and perhaps even a glimpse of miracle, spurred him onwards,  a single flickering candle against the vast darkness of the medieval world.

Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova gripped the controls of the 'Stardust Drifter', her knuckles white.  The jump to the Andromeda galaxy was a risky one, a leap of faith across uncharted nebulae and potential gravitational anomalies.  The crew, a motley collection of xeno-linguists, navigators and engineers, held their breath as the ship warped through space-time, the stars a swirling kaleidoscope of impossible colours.  The destination: Kepler-186f, a planet whispered to hold the key to interstellar peace, a gamble worth taking despite the unimaginable distances and the lurking threat of unknown entities.
