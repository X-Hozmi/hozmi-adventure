
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his mule onward.  He carried a chipped map, its faded ink hinting at the fabled city of Zerzura, said to hold untold riches and ancient secrets.  Each creak of the saddle, each rustle in the dense forest, held the promise of danger – bandits, wild beasts, or the unforgiving terrain itself.  His journey was fraught with peril, yet the lure of the unknown pushed him ever closer to his destiny, a testament to the human spirit's enduring quest for adventure.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the 'Stardust Drifter' through the shimmering nebula of Xylos.  Her destination: Kepler-186f, a planet rumored to harbor sentient life unlike anything encountered before.  The jump to hyperspace was a blur of colours and disorienting sensations, a stark contrast to the meticulously planned navigation through asteroid fields and gravitational anomalies.  Facing potential alien encounters, technological malfunctions, and the vast, uncharted expanse of the cosmos, Eva and her crew represented a new wave of explorers, pushing the boundaries of human reach into the unimaginable depths of space.
