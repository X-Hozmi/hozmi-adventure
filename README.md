
Sir Reginald, his chainmail gleaming under the midday sun, spurred his destrier onward.  The rutted track, barely a path through the dense Ardennes forest, offered little comfort.  Whispers of a lost city, brimming with forgotten magic, fueled his perilous journey.  Each creak of his saddle, each rustle in the undergrowth, hinted at both wonder and danger.  He'd faced bandits and treacherous swamps, but the lure of the unknown, the promise of glory and untold riches, pushed him ever deeper into the heart of the wild.

Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted her starship, *Stardust*, across the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her mission: to chart the unmapped sectors of the Andromeda galaxy, searching for habitable planets.  The vast emptiness of space, peppered with the ethereal beauty of distant stars and nebulas, was both breathtaking and terrifying.  The hum of the hyperdrive and the ever-present threat of asteroid fields were constant companions, as were the challenges of maintaining her ship's fragile systems lightyears from any form of rescue. The potential discoveries, however, far outweighed the risk.
