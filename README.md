
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the open plains as Sir Reginald, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his destrier onward.  The journey to the Sultan's court was proving treacherous; bandits lurked in the shadowed forests, and the whispering rumors of a plague-ridden village ahead chilled him more than the November air.  Each creak of his saddle, each clang of his sword against its scabbard, was a reminder of the perils and uncertainties that accompanied every league of this medieval pilgrimage.  He carried with him not only a King's message, but also the weight of a kingdom’s hope on his weary shoulders.


Centuries hence, Captain Eva Rostova stared out the viewport of the *Stardust Wanderer*.  The swirling nebula painted a breathtaking canvas outside, but the mission's gravity outweighed any aesthetic appreciation.  Their jump drive malfunctioned halfway to the Xylos system, leaving them adrift, rationing fuel, and desperately scanning for any sign of a habitable planet.  The vast, unforgiving expanse of space, despite its mesmerizing beauty, held the same chilling uncertainty as the medieval plains; a different kind of wilderness, but just as capable of swallowing a lone traveler whole.
