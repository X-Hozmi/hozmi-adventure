
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his weary horse onward.  He carried a king's ransom in a hidden pouch – a desperate gamble to reach the distant city of Antioch before the plague took him.  Each creak of the saddle, each rustle in the nearby woods, held the promise of danger; bandits, wild animals, and the ever-present threat of sickness stalked his every step.  His journey was a tapestry woven with hardship, fleeting moments of camaraderie with fellow travelers, and the constant gnawing fear of failure.


Centuries later, Elara navigated the swirling nebulae of the Andromeda Galaxy in the sleek, silver hull of the 'Stardust Drifter'. Her destination: Xylos, a planet rumored to hold the key to unlocking faster-than-light travel.  The cosmos held breathtaking beauty – swirling gas giants and diamond planets scattered like cosmic dust – but also deadly dangers: rogue asteroids, unpredictable wormholes, and the ever-present threat of pirate raids.  Each jump to hyperspace was a gamble, a leap of faith into the unknown, punctuated by the hum of the Alcubierre drive and the silent weight of her mission.
