
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his mule onward.  The journey to the distant monastery, a pilgrimage undertaken for his ailing sister, was fraught with peril.  Bandits lurked in the shadowed forests, rivers swelled with spring rains threatened to sweep him away, and the ever-present threat of disease hung heavy in the air. Each sunrise brought a renewed sense of determination, each sunset a prayer for safe passage through this unforgiving, yet breathtaking landscape.  The path ahead was uncertain, but the hope in his heart, fueled by his sister’s need, spurred him onward.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the 'Stardust Drifter' through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her destination: Kepler-186f, a potentially habitable exoplanet light-years from Earth.  Navigating asteroid fields and dodging the gravitational pull of rogue planets was a daily challenge, but the allure of discovery outweighed the risks.  The vast expanse of space, shimmering with cosmic dust and the distant glow of galaxies, was a testament to the limitless frontiers of human ambition.  With her crew, she was pushing the boundaries of known space, seeking not only new worlds, but a new understanding of the universe itself.
