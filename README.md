
In the medieval era, an adventure across land was an undertaking fraught with both peril and promise. Leaving the familiar confines of a village or city meant venturing into a world largely uncharted, where maps were crude and rumors often replaced facts. Travelers, whether merchants, pilgrims, knights, or outcasts, faced treacherous terrain, dense forests rumored to house mythical beasts, swift rivers demanding ferry or ford, and desolate plains where bandits lay in wait. Each mile was a testament to courage, a step further into the unknown, driven by the lure of trade, the call of faith, the pursuit of glory, or simply the desperate need for a new beginning.
