
The year is 1347.  Dust billowed behind Elara's mule as she traversed the unforgiving terrain of the Pyrenees.  Each creak of the saddle, each rustle in the sparse undergrowth, held a potential threat – bandits, wild animals, or the ever-present hunger.  Her worn map, a tattered testament to countless leagues already covered, offered little comfort against the vast unknown stretching before her; a journey to reach a mythical monastery rumored to hold the cure for her ailing brother was a gamble with life itself, a testament to the enduring human spirit's quest for hope amidst adversity.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the 'Stardust Drifter' through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her destination: Kepler-186f, a planet promising habitable conditions light years from Earth.  Navigating asteroid fields and encountering strange, alien phenomena was commonplace on her intergalactic voyages, a stark contrast to the terrestrial struggles of her medieval predecessors. Yet, the underlying drive remained the same – the relentless human desire to explore, to discover, and to push the boundaries of the known, regardless of the perils that lay ahead.
