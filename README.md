
The year is 1347.  Dust billowed behind Elara's mule as she crested the pass, the wind whipping her cloak around her like a frantic bird.  Before her stretched the endless expanse of the Holy Roman Empire, a tapestry woven with whispering forests, treacherous mountain passes, and the ever-present threat of bandit raids. Her quest, a pilgrimage to a rumored monastery holding a sacred relic, demanded unwavering courage and resilience.  Each day brought new challenges – navigating unfamiliar dialects, bartering for meager sustenance, and relying on the kindness (or fear) of strangers for shelter.

Centuries later, Captain Rexx slammed his fist on the console, the jarring sound barely audible above the hum of the Althea's warp drive.  Their journey to Xylos, a planet nestled in the Andromeda galaxy, was fraught with peril.  Nebulae shimmered like cosmic oil spills, threatening to snare their ship in their gravity wells, while unpredictable wormholes offered shortcuts fraught with the risk of temporal distortion.  The crew, a motley collection of explorers and mercenaries, faced not just the harsh realities of interstellar travel but also the ever-present danger of pirates and the unknown entities lurking in the vast darkness between the stars.
