
The year is 1347.  Dust choked the air as Sir Kaelan's battered warhorse stumbled onward, the scent of woodsmoke and decaying leaves clinging to the chill wind.  Their journey across the unforgiving Breton countryside was a tapestry woven with hardship:  bandits lurking in shadowed forests, treacherous mountain passes clinging to sheer cliffs, and the ever-present threat of starvation.  Yet, the lure of the mythical City of Ys, whispered to hold untold riches and ancient magic, propelled them forward, a flickering beacon in the encroaching darkness.  Each sunrise brought a new challenge, each sunset the bittersweet victory of survival.


Centuries hence, Captain Eva Rostova navigated the shimmering nebula of Xylos, her starship *Stardust* a tiny speck against the colossal expanse of the cosmos.  The journey to Kepler-186f, a planet promising habitable conditions, was measured not in days but in light-years.  Cosmic storms raged, threatening to tear apart their vessel, while the endless void pressed in, a constant reminder of their isolation.  But the possibility of discovering new life, of charting unmapped territories in the galaxy, fueled their courage, transforming the perils of interstellar travel into an exhilarating, if terrifying, adventure.
