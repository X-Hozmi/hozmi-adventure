
Sir Reginald, his chainmail glinting under the unforgiving midday sun, spurred his destrier onward.  The rough track, barely a path through the dense Ardennes forest, offered little respite from the relentless heat.  Ahead lay the rumored lost city of Eldoria, a place whispered about in hushed tones in taverns and castles alike, a city said to hold secrets both wondrous and terrifying.  Each creak of his saddle, each rustle in the undergrowth, fueled the thrilling uncertainty of his quest, a journey fraught with peril but promising untold riches and glory.

Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the starship *Stardust* through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her destination: Kepler-186f, a potentially habitable exoplanet light-years from Earth.  The vast emptiness of space, punctuated only by the distant pinpricks of stars, held a different kind of peril—the unpredictable nature of wormholes, the ever-present threat of solar flares, and the unknown dangers of alien civilizations.  But the promise of discovery, the chance to expand humanity's reach across the cosmos, was a siren song too powerful to resist.
