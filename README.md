
The year is 1347.  Dust choked the air as Sir Reginald, his armor gleaming faintly in the weak sun, urged his destrier onward.  The journey across the blighted lands to the King's summons was arduous; bandits lurked in the shadows of ruined castles, and the whispers of plague stalked the meager villages they passed.  Each creak of saddle leather, each splash of mud on his worn surcoat, marked another step closer to an uncertain destiny, a testament to the perilous beauty and constant threat of medieval travel.

Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova navigated the shimmering nebula, her starship, the *Starseeker*, a tiny speck against the infinite canvas of space.  The jump to Xylos Prime had been smoother than anticipated, but the sensor readings indicated a volatile energy signature nearby.   This was not a mere trade run; this was exploration into the unknown, a thrilling gamble against the vastness and potential dangers of interstellar travel, a journey pushing the boundaries of human ambition.
