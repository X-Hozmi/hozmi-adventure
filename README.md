Leaving the familiar confines of one's village meant confronting a world teeming with unknown dangers: dense, shadowed forests rumored to harbor mythical beasts, treacherous mountain passes where bandits lay in wait, and vast, desolate plains where the elements were the most formidable foe. Travelers, be they merchants seeking new markets, knights on quests for glory, or pilgrims journeying to holy sites, faced the constant threat of starvation, disease, and the ever-present uncertainty of what lay beyond the next bend in the forgotten road.
