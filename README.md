
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his mule onwards.  He clutched the worn map, its parchment brittle with age, depicting the perilous route to the mythical city of Ys.  Bandits, treacherous mountain passes, and the ever-present threat of starvation were but a few of the challenges awaiting him on this journey – a quest for fortune, or perhaps, just survival.  Each creak of the saddle, each rustle in the undergrowth, sent a jolt of adrenaline through him, fueling his weary body and driving him further towards the unknown.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the starship *Odyssey* through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her mission: to chart the unmapped regions of the Andromeda galaxy, searching for habitable planets amidst the celestial dust and colliding asteroids.  The vastness of space was both awe-inspiring and terrifying, a constant reminder of the fragility of life and the immense risks inherent in interstellar travel.  Each jump through hyperspace was a leap of faith, a gamble against the unpredictable nature of the cosmos, yet the promise of discovery propelled her ever onwards towards the next unexplored star system.
