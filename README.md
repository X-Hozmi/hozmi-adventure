
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the exposed plains as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his destrier onward.  He clutched the worn map, its parchment brittle with age, detailing a rumored shortcut to Constantinople – a shortcut rumored to be guarded by brigands and mythical beasts.  Each creak of the saddle, each rustle in the long grass, sent a shiver down his spine, a thrilling mixture of fear and anticipation as he pressed towards the unknown, driven by whispered tales of unimaginable riches and the promise of glory.


Centuries later, Captain Elara Reyes piloted the starship *Stardust*, its hyperdrive humming a deep, resonant thrum.  Beyond the viewport, a swirling nebula painted the void in hues of amethyst and emerald.  Her mission: to chart the unexplored Xylos sector, a region whispered to hold the key to faster-than-light travel – and possibly, to ancient, forgotten civilizations.  The vastness of space, both terrifying and exhilarating, pressed against her as she navigated through asteroid fields and navigated political minefields just as treacherous as any medieval battlefield.
