
The year is 1347.  Dust billowed behind Elara's mule as she crested the hill, the vast, unforgiving landscape of the Anatolian plateau stretching before her.  Her worn leather satchel held more than just supplies; it contained the hopes of her family, a desperate journey to Constantinople seeking refuge from the encroaching plague.  Each creak of the saddle, each rustle in the sparse brush, held the potential for both danger and discovery – bandits, starvation, or perhaps the unexpected kindness of a passing stranger. This land journey was a gamble, but survival itself was the ultimate prize.


Centuries later, Captain Rexa Kael navigated the shimmering nebula of Xylos, her starship a tiny speck against the cosmic canvas.  The journey to the Andromeda galaxy had already consumed years, a relentless push against the fabric of spacetime.  Ahead lay Kepler-186f, a planet rumored to hold the key to interstellar peace, a prize worth risking the dangers of uncharted space, hostile alien encounters, and the constant threat of engine failure. The vastness of the cosmos held infinite possibilities, both wondrous and terrifying.
