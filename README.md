
The year is 1347.  Sir Reginald, his surcoat stained with mud and sweat, urged his weary horse onward.  The scent of woodsmoke and damp earth hung heavy in the air, a stark contrast to the metallic tang of blood clinging to his sword.  His quest – to reach the mythical city of Eldoria, whispered to hold the cure for his ailing sister – had led him through treacherous mountain passes, across vast, sun-baked plains teeming with bandits, and through dark forests haunted by whispered legends.  Each sunrise brought new perils, each sunset a precarious victory in a relentless struggle against the elements and unscrupulous men.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova navigated the Xylos nebula, her starship, the *Stardust Drifter*, a tiny speck against the swirling cosmic dust.  The journey to Kepler-186f, a potential new colony world, was measured not in days or weeks, but in months of painstaking hyperspace jumps and meticulous course corrections.  The vastness of space, though beautiful, held its own dangers: rogue asteroids, unpredictable gravitational wells, and the ever-present threat of pirate raiders preying on vulnerable transports.  But the promise of a new dawn for humanity, a new home amongst the stars, fueled her courage and kept her ever vigilant on her perilous voyage.
