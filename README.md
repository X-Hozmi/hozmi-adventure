
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his mule onward.  The scent of woodsmoke and damp earth clung to the air, a stark contrast to the rumors of spice and silks that fueled his journey eastward.  He’d traded his meager inheritance for a map promising untold riches in the fabled city of Samarkand, a gamble against bandits, disease, and the ever-present gnawing fear of the unknown that lay beyond each sun-drenched hill.  Each day was a battle against fatigue and doubt, yet the lure of adventure, the whisper of distant lands, propelled him relentlessly forward.


Centuries later, Captain Elara Vance piloted the 'Stardust Wanderer' through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her destination: Kepler-186f, a potential colony world light-years from Earth.  The hum of the hyperdrive was a constant companion, a comforting drone against the vast, star-dusted emptiness.  This was not a journey for profit or glory, but for survival.  Humanity, having exhausted Earth's resources, was venturing into the cosmic unknown, risking everything for a chance at a new beginning.  Elara, her gaze fixed on the navigation console, felt the weight of that responsibility – the future of her species resting on the smooth precision of her hands and the unwavering strength of her ship.
