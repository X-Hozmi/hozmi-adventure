
The year is 1347.  Our band of weary travelers, cloaked in the dust of a thousand miles, crested the hill. Below, the sprawling city of Constantinople shimmered under the midday sun, a beacon after weeks traversing treacherous mountain passes and unforgiving plains. We had faced bandits, starvation, and the relentless bite of winter, but the thrill of reaching our destination, of witnessing the wonders of the Eastern Empire, far outweighed the hardship. Each scar, each blister, was a testament to the journey, a badge of honor earned on the unforgiving roads of medieval Europe.

Alternatively, imagine the hum of the Althea's warp drive, a constant lullaby against the backdrop of the inky blackness.  Our destination: Xylos, a jewel-toned planet orbiting a binary sun a million lightyears from Earth.  Years have melted into months, spent traversing the vast expanse of the Andromeda Galaxy. We've dodged asteroid fields, navigated nebulae of breathtaking beauty, and even encountered alien life—some friendly, some decidedly less so. But the promise of Xylos, a world whispered about in legend, spurs us onward, a beacon in the endless cosmos promising untold discoveries and unimaginable wonders.
