
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the plains as Sir Reginald, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his weary horse onward.  The journey from York to the Holy Land felt endless, a tapestry woven with the harsh beauty of untamed landscapes.  Each sunrise brought new challenges: treacherous mountain passes, muddy rivers to ford, and the ever-present threat of bandits lurking in shadowed forests.  The promise of Jerusalem, however, fueled his relentless pace, a beacon guiding him through hardship and uncertainty.  He carried with him not only his sword but also the weight of a vow, a pilgrimage as perilous as it was sacred.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova navigated the shimmering nebula of Xylos, her starship, the *Stardust Wanderer*, a tiny speck against the vast cosmic canvas.  Faster-than-light travel blurred the lines between destinations; planets became mere waypoints in a grand intergalactic voyage.  While the physical dangers were different – asteroid fields, rogue black holes, and the unpredictable nature of alien encounters – the spirit of adventure remained unchanged. The unknown stretched before her, a universe of possibilities and peril, fuelled by the insatiable human desire to explore the boundless reaches of space.
