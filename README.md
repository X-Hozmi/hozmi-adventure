
Sir Reginald, his surcoat stained with mud and sweat, spurred his destrier onward.  The rough track, barely a path through the dense Ardennes forest, offered little respite from the relentless sun.  Each creak of his saddle, each rustle in the undergrowth, heightened the sense of peril.  Rumors of brigands and worse haunted his journey to the distant kingdom of Aquitaine, a quest as perilous as any dragon's hoard. He clutched the King's missive, the weight of his responsibility a physical burden matched only by the weight of his pack.

Captain Eva Rostova gripped the controls of the 'Stardust Drifter', the nebula of Xylos swirling outside her viewport.  Years she’d spent charting this unexplored sector, navigating asteroid fields and avoiding the territorial claims of the Kryll Hegemony.  The destination: Kepler-186f, a planet rumored to hold the key to interstellar faster-than-light travel.  The risks were immense, the potential rewards unimaginable, and the loneliness of space a constant companion in this vast, unknowable expanse.
