
Sir Reginald, his chainmail gleaming under the harsh sun, spurred his destrier onward.  The road, a barely-there track winding through the unforgiving Breton countryside, offered little respite from the dust and the ever-present threat of bandits.  Each creak of his saddle, each rustle in the nearby woods, sent a prickle of apprehension down his spine.  He carried a king's ransom – and a king's orders – to deliver to the besieged city of Nantes, a journey fraught with peril, but one he had vowed to complete, come what may. The thrill of the unknown, the challenge of survival against the odds, fueled his weary muscles.

Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the 'Stardust Wanderer' through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  The jump to the Andromeda galaxy had been treacherous, pushing the ship's Alcubierre drive to its limits.  Navigating asteroid fields and evading the predatory K'tharr cruisers was a daily routine, each successful hyperspace jump a victory against the vastness and the dangers of interstellar travel. The allure of discovery, the promise of new worlds and civilizations beyond human comprehension, outweighed the risks a thousandfold.  Her mission: to find the legendary lost colony of Kepler-186f.
