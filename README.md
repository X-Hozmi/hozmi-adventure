
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his mule onward.  His knapsack, heavy with meager supplies and a tattered map, felt like a lead weight on his back.  He was bound for the fabled city of Ys, whispered to hold secrets of immense power, a journey fraught with peril – bandits, treacherous mountain passes, and the ever-present threat of starvation.  Each sunrise brought a renewed hope, tempered by the knowledge that survival itself was the greatest adventure.


Millennia later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the 'Stardust Wanderer' through the swirling nebula of Xylos, a breathtaking tapestry of crimson and gold.  Her mission: to chart a new hyperspace route to the Andromeda galaxy, a journey that could take generations.  The vastness of space held both wonder and terror; the silent hum of the hyperdrive engine a constant reminder of their isolation, a testament to humanity's relentless quest to explore the uncharted cosmos.  The true adventure lay not just in reaching the destination, but in the unfathomable discoveries that awaited along the way.
