
**Medieval Era (Travel Across Land)**

Embarking on an adventure in the medieval era meant stepping into a world largely untamed, where maps were rudimentary and the horizon often held more mystery than certainty. Whether a knight on a quest, a merchant seeking distant markets, or a pilgrim bound for a holy site, travel across land was a monumental undertaking, often on foot, horseback, or by creaking cart. Each journey unfolded through dense forests, across treacherous mountain passes, and over vast, open plains, with every mile revealing new landscapes and the promise of untold experiences beyond the familiar village borders.

Such expeditions were fraught with peril: encounters with brigands, the threat of wild beasts, the unpredictable wrath of nature, and the ever-present risk of illness far from home.
