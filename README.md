
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his mule onward.  The journey from York to Constantinople felt endless, a tapestry woven with the threat of bandits, treacherous mountain passes, and the ever-present gnawing hunger. Each sunrise brought the promise of new dangers – disease in a crowded village, a river swollen with spring rains, or the unsettling silence of a forest where wolves howled unseen.  Yet, the lure of the exotic East, whispers of silks and spices, spurred him relentlessly onward.

Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova gripped the controls of the *Stardust Drifter*, the vibrant nebula of the Crab Cluster a breathtaking spectacle outside her viewport.  Their mission: to chart the unexplored regions of the Andromeda Galaxy, a journey spanning lightyears and untold risks.  Navigating asteroid fields, dodging the gravitational pull of rogue planets, and facing the ever-present threat of spatial anomalies demanded unwavering skill and courage.  But the potential discovery of new life, new worlds, fueled their relentless exploration across the vast cosmic ocean.
