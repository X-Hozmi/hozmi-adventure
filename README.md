
The year is 1347.  Dust choked the air as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin clinging to his sweat-soaked skin, spurred his mount onward.  The midday sun beat down mercilessly on the parched landscape of Provence, the distant Pyrenees a hazy promise of cooler climes.  Bandits, rumored to lurk in the shadowed gorges, were a constant threat, as was the ever-present hunger.  Each creak of his saddle, each rustle in the dry grass, sent a jolt of adrenaline through him – a thrilling, terrifying dance between survival and the lure of the unknown that lay beyond the mountains.  His quest, a pilgrimage to Santiago de Compostela, was a perilous gamble, but the whispered legends of miracles and the thirst for adventure fueled his weary steps.


Centuries later, Elara's starship, *Stardust Drifter*, cut a silent path through the nebula NGC 7000.  The swirling colours of cosmic dust painted breathtaking murals across her viewport, a stark contrast to the cold, precise hum of the engines.  Her mission: to chart unmapped sectors of the Andromeda galaxy, a journey spanning light-years and fraught with the perils of uncharted space.  Navigating asteroid fields, deciphering cryptic alien signals, and facing the ever-present threat of solar flares, Elara, a lone explorer in the vast emptiness, felt a kinship with those medieval pilgrims –  the same insatiable hunger for discovery driving them both, separated only by the vast gulf of time and technology.
