
The year is 1347.  Our weary band, a motley collection of knight, scholar, and thief, trudged onward, the muddy track a testament to weeks spent traversing the unforgiving French countryside.  Each sunrise revealed new challenges – bandits lurking in shadowed forests, treacherous mountain passes slick with rain, and the ever-present threat of starvation.  Yet, the allure of the rumored lost city of Ys, whispered to hold untold riches and ancient secrets, propelled us ever forward, a beacon burning bright against the backdrop of a brutal, yet breathtaking, world.

Conversely, in the 27th century, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the 'Stardust Wanderer' through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her mission: to locate and secure a rare element vital to interstellar travel, nestled on a planet orbiting a dying star a million light-years from Earth.  The journey, fraught with the dangers of asteroid fields, hostile alien encounters, and the ever-present threat of warp core failure, was a test of skill and courage. But the breathtaking views of celestial wonders, alien worlds teeming with life (and death), made the incredible risks worthwhile.
