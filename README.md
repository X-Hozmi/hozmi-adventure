
The year is 1347.  Dust billowed behind Elara's mule as she crested the hill, the sun bleaching the ochre landscape of Provence.  Ahead lay the Pyrenees, a daunting barrier between her and the fabled city of Santiago de Compostela.  Each day brought the threat of bandits, starvation, or disease, but the allure of pilgrimage, the whispered promise of miracles, fueled her onward.  She shared meager meals with fellow travelers, their stories weaving a tapestry of hope and hardship, a testament to the enduring human spirit seeking adventure amidst the medieval world’s harsh realities.

Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova navigated the Xylos Nebula in her starship, *Stardust Drifter*.  Vast, swirling nebulas were commonplace, but the Xylos held a unique energy signature, potentially the key to faster-than-light travel.  Each jump between star systems was a gamble, a calculated risk against unknown gravitational forces and the ever-present threat of space pirates.  Yet, the promise of galactic exploration, the chance to discover new worlds and civilizations, outweighed the dangers.  The cosmos, boundless and perilous, beckoned with its myriad possibilities.
