Journeys across the land were epic undertakings, fraught with dangers that tested the mettle of any traveler. From the lone knight seeking glory or a quest, to the merchant caravan laden with exotic goods, or even pilgrims seeking redemption, every path was a gamble. Travelers navigated by crude maps, ancient tales, or the guidance of seasoned, albeit superstitious, guides, their path illuminated only by the sun, moon, or the flickering torchlight in a roadside inn. Encounters with foreign cultures, noble lords, desperate peasants, or even dark sorcery were not uncommon, shaping destinies and forging legends with every step. The promise of rediscovered relics, bountiful trade, a distant princess's hand, or simply the thrill of seeing what lay beyond the next horizon fueled these epic voyages, transforming ordinary folk into unwitting heroes of their own unfolding sagas.
