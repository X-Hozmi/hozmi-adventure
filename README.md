
The year is 1347.  Dust billowed behind Elara's horse as she urged it onward, the setting sun painting the rugged terrain in hues of blood orange and bruised purple.  Her worn leather satchel held her meager possessions and a tattered map promising untold riches in the faraway city of Samarkand – a journey fraught with peril, from brigands lurking in shadowed forests to the unpredictable whims of nature.  Each creak of the saddle, each rustle in the undergrowth, held the promise of excitement or disaster, a constant reminder of the unforgiving yet alluring nature of medieval travel.


Centuries later, Captain Rexx slammed the controls of the 'Stardust Drifter', the hyperdrive spitting them out near the Andromeda Galaxy.  Navigation through the swirling nebulae was a delicate dance, a test of skill and nerve against the crushing gravity of celestial bodies and unpredictable wormholes. The mission, to retrieve a rare element vital for terraforming, demanded courage, precision, and a healthy dose of luck in the face of interstellar hazards.  The vast emptiness of space, breathtaking in its scale, was both an inspiring canvas and a silent, ever-present threat.
