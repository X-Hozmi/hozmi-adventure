
The year is 1347.  Sir Reginald, his worn leather jerkin creaking with every stride, urged his weary mount onward.  The chill wind whipped across the desolate moors, a stark contrast to the tales of vibrant cities and opulent kingdoms that fueled his journey.  Bandits, treacherous passes, and the ever-present threat of starvation were constant companions, yet the glint of the promised riches – and the honor of fulfilling his King's quest – pushed him ever forward, across a tapestry of muddy tracks, bustling market towns, and silent forests echoing with the whispers of ancient legends.

Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the 'Stardust Wanderer' through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her destination: Kepler-186f, a planet rumored to hold the key to interstellar travel's next great leap.  Navigating asteroid fields and dodging the gravitational pull of rogue planets was child's play compared to the political intrigue and corporate espionage that shadowed her mission.  But the potential discovery, the sheer breathtaking expanse of the cosmos surrounding her, and the possibility of expanding human knowledge – these were rewards worth risking everything for.
