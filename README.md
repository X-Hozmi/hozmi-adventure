
The year is 1347.  Dust choked the air as Sir Kaelan's worn leather boots crunched on the rutted track.  His journey, a pilgrimage to the shrine of Saint Elara, had taken him through sun-baked plains, treacherous mountain passes shrouded in mist, and dark forests teeming with bandits and wild beasts.  Each sunrise brought new challenges – navigating by the stars, bartering for meager provisions in forgotten villages, and constantly being wary of the ever-present threat of conflict. His quest was a test of endurance, faith, and the resilience of the human spirit, a testament to the unpredictable beauty and harsh realities of medieval travel.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the 'Stardust Drifter' through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her mission: to retrieve vital bio-samples from the Kepler-186f system, a journey spanning lightyears and demanding precise navigation through asteroid fields and gravitational anomalies.  The vastness of space, both awe-inspiring and terrifying, pressed upon her.  Communication delays with Earth felt like an eternity, and the responsibility for her crew's safety weighed heavily. Yet, the lure of discovery, the uncharted territories of the cosmos, spurred her onward, a modern-day explorer pushing the boundaries of human knowledge and capability.
