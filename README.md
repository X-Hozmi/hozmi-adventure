
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his mule onward.  The journey to the fabled city of Serenia had already claimed several weeks, each dawn revealing a new challenge: treacherous mountain passes guarded by bandits, swollen rivers demanding perilous crossings, and nights spent huddled around meager fires under the vast, indifferent stars.  His quest, to find a cure for his ailing sister, fuelled his weary limbs and hardened his resolve against the relentless hardships of the medieval world.  The whispers of Serenia's healing springs were his only compass, guiding him through the unknown.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the starship *Stardust* across the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her destination: Kepler-186f, a potentially habitable exoplanet light-years from Earth.  The journey, measured not in weeks but in months of warp speed travel, was a symphony of calculations and controlled chaos.  Navigating asteroid fields, evading rogue space pirates, and maintaining the delicate balance of life support were everyday occurrences.  Yet, the allure of discovery, the hope of finding new life and expanding humanity’s reach across the cosmos, outweighed the inherent risks of interstellar travel in this vibrant, unforgiving future.
