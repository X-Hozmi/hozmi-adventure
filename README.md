
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the desolate plains as Sir Kaelan and his small band pressed onward, their horses' hooves sinking into the mud.  Their quest: to reach the mythical city of Eldoria, rumored to hold a cure for the plague ravaging their homeland.  Each day brought new perils – bandits lurking in shadowed forests, treacherous mountain passes, and the ever-present threat of starvation.  Yet, the lure of Eldoria, and the hope it represented, fueled their weary steps across the vast, unforgiving landscape of medieval Europe.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the 'Stardust Drifter' through the swirling nebula of Xylos. Her mission: a daring survey of a newly discovered galaxy, brimming with unexplored planets and the promise of unimaginable resources.  Navigating asteroid fields and evading the unpredictable gravitational pull of black holes was child's play compared to the political maneuvering back on Earth. But the vast emptiness of space held a different kind of peril – the chilling solitude of the cosmos, and the ever-present unknown waiting in the black.
