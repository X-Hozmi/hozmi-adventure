
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his horse onward.  The scent of woodsmoke and damp earth filled his nostrils, a stark contrast to the tales whispered in taverns – of shimmering cities in the East, of silks finer than spun moonlight, and of dragons guarding mountains of gold.  Each creak of his saddle, each rumble of his horse's hooves, was a beat in the drum of his grand adventure, a journey fraught with peril, but brimming with the promise of untold riches and glory.  He'd face bandits, treacherous passes, and the ever-present threat of starvation, but the lure of the unknown spurred him on.


Centuries later,  Captain Eva Rostova stared out at the swirling nebula, a kaleidoscope of cosmic dust and dying stars, from the bridge of the *Starseeker*.  Her destination: Kepler-186f, a potentially habitable exoplanet light-years from Earth.  The hum of the Alcubierre drive was a constant companion, a testament to humanity's mastery of interstellar travel.  Yet, the vastness of space, the unpredictable nature of wormholes, and the ever-present threat of pirate raids made this journey as fraught with danger as any medieval expedition.  The quest for new worlds, for resources, and for knowledge, demanded courage, skill, and a healthy dose of unwavering hope.
