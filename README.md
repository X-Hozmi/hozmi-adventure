
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his mule onwards.  The journey from York to Constantinople felt endless, a tapestry woven with the threat of brigands, the discomfort of relentless rain, and the intoxicating allure of the unknown. Each sunrise brought new challenges: navigating treacherous mountain passes, bartering for meager provisions in dusty villages, and sharing stories around crackling fires with fellow travelers, their tales of far-off lands fueling his own restless spirit.  The ultimate prize?  A rumored lost city, whispered to hold secrets of unimaginable power.

Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the *Stardust Drifter* through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her destination: Kepler-186f, a potentially habitable exoplanet light-years from Earth.  The journey was a symphony of calculated risks and technological marvels – hyperspace jumps that bent spacetime, automated repairs fought against the corrosive effects of interstellar dust, and the ever-present threat of pirate raiders preying on slower vessels. Yet, the vast, inky canvas of space, studded with diamonds of distant suns, held a far greater allure than any earthly landscape ever could, whispering promises of new discoveries and the potential for a future among the stars.
