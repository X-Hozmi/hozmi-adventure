
In the medieval era, venturing across land was an adventure woven with both peril and profound discovery. Beyond the familiar cobblestones of a town or the comforting security of a castle, lay a vast, untamed continent. Journeys for trade, pilgrimage, or conquest meant days or weeks spent on ancient, often unmaintained roads, facing the constant threat of banditry, treacherous weather, and the sheer enormity of a wilderness where accurate maps were scarce and reliable waypoints even rarer. Every rustle in the forest, every distant silhouette, and every uncharted village offered a mix of potential danger and untold experiences.

This arduous travel, however, forged the very spirit of adventure.
