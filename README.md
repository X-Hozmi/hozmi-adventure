
In the medieval era, an adventure across land was not merely a journey, but an odyssey into the untamed heart of the world. Leaving the familiar cobblestones of a fortified town, a traveler might embark on a quest for distant trade, a holy pilgrimage to a sacred shrine, or a perilous mission for their lord. Each step on the muddy track or through ancient, whispering forests carried the weight of the unknown, with distant mountain ranges shimmering like promises or threats on the horizon, and the map often a vague, hand-drawn sketch rather than a reliable guide.
