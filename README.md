
The year is 1347.  Dust choked the air as Elara’s worn boots pounded the rutted track, the midday sun beating down on her and her mule laden with meager supplies.  Their journey across the Duchy of Aquitaine was fraught with peril; bandits lurked in shadowed forests, rivers swelled with unpredictable fury, and the ever-present threat of disease hung heavy. Yet, with each sunrise, Elara pressed on, driven by a desperate hope to reach the fabled healer in Avignon, a journey as perilous as it was necessary. The landscape, a tapestry of sun-baked fields and looming castles, both threatened and beguiled her, each step a gamble against the unpredictable odds of medieval travel.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the *Starseeker* through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her destination: Kepler-186f, a potentially habitable exoplanet light-years from Earth. The journey was a symphony of calculated risk, navigating asteroid fields and wormholes, the ship a tiny vessel against the immense scale of the cosmos.  Each jump through hyperspace was a leap of faith, a gamble against unforeseen gravitational anomalies and rogue energy bursts.  But the promise of discovery, the thrill of charting unmapped territories among the stars, fueled her unwavering determination.
