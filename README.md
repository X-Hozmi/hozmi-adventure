
The year is 1347.  A chill wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his pack heavy with meager supplies, urged his weary horse onward.  The journey from York to the Holy Land felt endless, a tapestry woven with the dangers of bandits, treacherous terrain, and the ever-present threat of disease.  Each sunrise brought the promise of another day's struggle, yet the fervent hope of reaching Jerusalem fueled his relentless stride, pushing him through exhaustion and despair.  He dreamt not of riches, but of redemption, a pilgrimage etched in sweat and hardship.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the *Stardust Drifter* through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her mission: to retrieve a vital data core from the abandoned research station on Kepler-186f.  The jump to hyperspace had been exhilarating, a breathtaking leap across unimaginable distances, yet the quiet hum of the ship's engines couldn't mask the gnawing loneliness of deep space travel.  Ahead lay the unknown – the perilous landing on a potentially hostile planet, the possibility of encountering alien life, and the ever-present threat of equipment failure light years from any rescue.  Her courage, however, burned as bright as the distant stars.
