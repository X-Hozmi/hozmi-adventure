
The year is 1347.  Dust coated Sir Reginald’s surcoat as he urged his weary mount onward, the setting sun painting the endless plains of France in hues of blood orange and bruised purple.  His quest: to reach the fabled monastery of Saint Fiacre, rumored to hold the cure for his ailing sister.  Each creak of saddle leather, each rustle in the tall grass, held the promise of adventure, or peril – bandits, wild beasts, and the ever-present threat of illness lurking in the shadows of the untamed landscape.  He rode on, his heart a mixture of fear and fierce determination.

Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the *Stardust Wanderer* through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her destination: Kepler-186f, a potential colony world light years from Earth.  The vastness of space, a breathtaking panorama of celestial wonders and lurking dangers, was as challenging and unpredictable as any medieval forest.  Navigating asteroid fields, evading pirate ships, and dealing with the ever-present threat of engine failure all formed part of the cosmic odyssey, a thrilling voyage into the uncharted territories of the galaxy, fueled by hope for a better future among the stars.
