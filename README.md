
The year is 1347.  My worn leather boots crunched on the frozen ground, each step a victory against the biting wind that whipped across the desolate plains.  Ahead lay the shadowed peaks of the Dragon's Teeth mountains, a perilous path to the fabled city of Eldoria, rumored to hold a cure for the plague ravaging my homeland.  With a meager pouch of coins and a heart full of hope (and a healthy dose of fear), I pressed on, my trusty mule the only companion against the vast, unforgiving landscape – a testament to the unpredictable and often brutal beauty of medieval travel.


Alternatively, in 2742, the hum of the Althea's hyperdrive was a constant, comforting thrum against the backdrop of the inky black.  Millions of stars blazed outside my viewport, each a potential destination, a new world waiting to be explored. This voyage to Xylos, a planet whispered to hold the key to interstellar communication, felt less a journey and more a breathtaking leap across the infinite canvas of the cosmos.  The risks were unimaginable, the rewards potentially limitless, a stark contrast to the grounded realities of Earth, yet echoing the same human drive for discovery that pushed explorers across medieval maps.
