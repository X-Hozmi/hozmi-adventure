
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the plains as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his weary horse onward.  The map, a crudely drawn parchment, promised riches in the distant Sultanate of Delhi – silks, spices, and enough gold to buy a small castle.  But the path was fraught with peril: bandits lurked in the shadowed forests, rivers swelled with spring rains threatened to sweep him away, and the constant threat of disease hung heavy in the air.  Each sunrise brought new challenges, each sunset a fragile victory in a race against the elements and the unknown.

Centuries later, Elara piloted the 'Stardust Drifter' through the swirling nebula of Xylos. Her destination: Kepler-186f, a promising exoplanet rumored to hold the key to faster-than-light travel.  Navigating the treacherous asteroid fields and avoiding the predatory space krakens was a daily struggle, but the allure of unlocking interstellar travel, of charting unmapped galaxies, fuelled her relentless drive. The vast emptiness of space offered both breathtaking beauty and terrifying isolation, a constant reminder of the fragility of life against the backdrop of infinity.
