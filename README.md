For merchants, pilgrims, knights, and even ordinary folk seeking new horizons, every journey across the vast, untamed tapestry of dense forests, treacherous mountains, and sprawling plains became an epic undertaking. Beyond the familiar borders of one's village lay lands shrouded in myth and rumour, where the next settlement could be days away, making each deliberate step into the unknown a true test of courage and resolve.

These overland expeditions were a profound test of endurance and wit, demanding resilience against the elements, vigilance against brigands, and the constant threat of the wild. Travelers might navigate by sun and stars, relying on rudimentary maps or local knowledge to cross winding rivers and impenetrable fens.
