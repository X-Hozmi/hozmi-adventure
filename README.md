
The year is 1347.  Dust swirled around the worn leather boots of Thomas as he urged his weary mule onward.  The shimmering heat haze distorted the endless expanse of the French countryside, a far cry from the cozy familiarity of his village.  His quest, a pilgrimage to Santiago de Compostela, had transformed from a pious journey into a brutal test of endurance.  Bandits lurked in the shadows of ancient forests, and the constant threat of disease and starvation gnawed at his resolve. Yet, with each sunrise, the allure of the distant cathedral, a beacon of hope, spurred him relentlessly forward.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova gripped the controls of the 'Stardust Wanderer', the hum of the hyperdrive a constant thrum beneath her feet.  Her destination: Xylos, a planet shrouded in mystery light-years from the colonized sectors of the Andromeda galaxy.  The journey, a blur of nebulae and distant star clusters, was punctuated by the occasional asteroid field – a thrilling, if occasionally terrifying, dance with fate.   Each jump through hyperspace held the promise of discovery, but also the chilling possibility of encountering something far beyond human comprehension.
