
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his weary horse onward.  The journey to the Holy Land had been fraught with peril – bandits lurking in shadowed forests, treacherous mountain passes, and the ever-present threat of disease.  Each sunrise brought a fresh challenge, from bartering for meager sustenance in dusty villages to navigating by the stars under an unforgiving sky. Yet, the lure of Jerusalem, the whispered promises of divine grace, spurred him onward, his heart filled with a mixture of fear and fervent hope.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the *Stardust Drifter* through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her mission: to retrieve a vital bio-sample from the planet Xylos-7, a world teeming with exotic flora and potentially hostile lifeforms.  Warp jumps between star systems were commonplace, but navigating asteroid fields and avoiding pirate vessels remained a constant threat.  The vastness of space, both awe-inspiring and terrifying, offered breathtaking views of celestial wonders, a stark contrast to the ever-present danger lurking just beyond the ship's shields.
