
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his mule onward.  He was bound for Constantinople, a journey fraught with peril. Bandit ambushes in the shadowed forests, the ever-present threat of disease in crowded towns, and the unpredictable nature of the weather all loomed large.  Each sunrise brought a new challenge, from navigating treacherous mountain passes to bartering for meager sustenance in hostile villages.  His quest, to find a lost scroll rumored to hold the key to an ancient secret, fueled his determination against the overwhelming odds.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the 'Stardust Wanderer' through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her mission: to chart unexplored sectors of the Andromeda galaxy, searching for habitable planets. The journey was a tapestry woven from the breathtaking beauty of cosmic wonders and the chilling reality of navigating asteroid fields and encountering unknown alien lifeforms.  Faster-than-light travel offered incredible speed, but the isolation of interstellar space and the constant hum of the ship's engines were a constant reminder of the vast distances and immense risks inherent in their galactic exploration.
