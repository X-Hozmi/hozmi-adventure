
Sir Reginald, his worn leather jerkin creaking with every stride, squinted at the sun-baked track ahead.  The journey across the Dornish marches had been brutal; bandits, scorching heat, and the ever-present threat of wild beasts had tested his mettle and his meager supplies. But the whispers of a lost city, brimming with forgotten magic, spurred him onward.  Each creak of his saddle, each clang of his sword against his thigh, was a drumbeat of anticipation for the unknown treasures and perils that lay beyond the next ridge.

Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted her starship, *The Wanderer*, through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her mission: to chart unexplored sectors of the Andromeda Galaxy, a task fraught with dangers both natural – rogue asteroids, gravitational anomalies – and potentially artificial, the lingering whispers of long-vanished civilizations.  The vastness of space, a breathtaking and terrifying expanse of glittering dust and dark matter, dwarfed even the most audacious dreams, but the promise of discovery, of unraveling the universe's deepest mysteries, was a fuel far more potent than any fusion reactor.
