
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his mule onward.  The scent of woodsmoke and damp earth mingled with the metallic tang of fear. His journey to the distant city of Prague, a pilgrimage to seek a cure for his ailing sister, was fraught with peril. Bandits lurked in the shadowed forests, and the very roads were treacherous, pitted and strewn with obstacles.  Each sunrise brought a new challenge – a ford to cross, a mountain pass to navigate, a village to cautiously approach.  His only companions were his dwindling supplies and the unwavering hope in his heart.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the *Stardust Drifter* through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her mission: to retrieve a vital data core from the abandoned research station on Kepler-186f, a planet light years from Earth.  Navigating asteroid fields and battling solar flares was routine, but the unknown dangers of a derelict alien installation, potentially housing hostile lifeforms or malfunctioning technology, presented a far greater threat. The vastness of space, both beautiful and terrifying, stretched before her, a testament to humanity's bold ambition and the inherent risks of galactic exploration.
