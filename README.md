
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his pack heavy with meager supplies, pressed onward.  His journey across the Kingdom of Francia was fraught with peril – bandits lurking in shadowed forests, treacherous mountain passes, and the ever-present threat of starvation.  Each sunrise brought the promise of new challenges: navigating unfamiliar dialects, bartering for food and shelter, and always, the looming fear of the unknown, a constant companion on his arduous pilgrimage to the Holy Land.  Yet, with every hardship overcome, a sense of exhilaration grew, fueled by the thrill of adventure and the burning desire to reach his destination.


Centuries later,  Captain Eva Rostova piloted the *Stardust Drifter* through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her mission: to chart unexplored sectors of the Andromeda Galaxy, a journey that spanned light-years and defied the limitations of human endurance.  The vastness of space was both terrifying and mesmerizing;  each new planet offered a unique and often hostile environment, demanding ingenuity and courage to overcome.  Alien landscapes, encounters with unknown species, and the constant threat of malfunctioning technology – all were part of the intoxicating blend of danger and discovery that defined her existence as a pioneer of the cosmos.
