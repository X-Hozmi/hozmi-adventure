
The year is 1347.  Sir Reginald, his armor gleaming faintly under the relentless sun, spurred his destrier onwards.  The rutted track, barely a path through the dense Ardennes forest, offered little respite from the oppressive heat.  Days bled into weeks, marked only by the shifting landscapes – sun-drenched meadows giving way to shadowy forests, then rocky passes overlooking vast, windswept plains.  Each sunrise brought the gnawing uncertainty of bandits, wild animals, and the ever-present threat of disease, but the lure of the fabled city of Eldoria, whispered to hold untold riches and powerful magic, kept him pushing forward, his spirit undimmed.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the *Starseeker* through the swirling nebula, a kaleidoscope of cosmic dust and dying stars.  Her destination: Kepler-186f, a potentially habitable planet light-years from Earth.  The journey, a relentless test of both ship and crew, had already spanned months, each day a delicate dance between navigating treacherous asteroid fields, repairing critical systems, and battling the soul-crushing loneliness of interstellar space.  Yet, the potential discovery of extraterrestrial life, a new beginning for humanity, fueled their unwavering determination.
