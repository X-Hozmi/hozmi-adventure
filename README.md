
The year is 1347.  A chill wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his horse onward.  He was bound for Constantinople, a journey fraught with peril.  Bandits lurked in the shadowed forests, disease stalked the crowded towns, and the unpredictable weather threatened to halt his progress entirely.  Each sunrise brought a new challenge – navigating treacherous mountain passes, fording swollen rivers, or bartering for meager provisions in dusty villages. Yet, the lure of the exotic East, the whispered tales of unimaginable riches and wonders, spurred him on.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the starship *Stardust* across the void between galaxies.  Her destination: Xylos, a planet rumored to hold the key to faster-than-light travel.  The journey was measured not in days, but in light-years, a relentless dance with gravity wells and unpredictable nebulae.  The crew faced the ever-present threat of equipment failure, cosmic radiation, and the unknown dangers lurking in uncharted space.  But the promise of unlocking the universe’s deepest secrets, the possibility of contacting other civilizations, was a reward worth risking everything for.
