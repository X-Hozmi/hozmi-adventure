
The year is 1347.  Dust swirled around Sir Gareth's worn boots as he spurred his destrier onward, the setting sun painting the endless fields of France a fiery orange.  His quest: to reach the legendary city of Ys, rumored to lie hidden beneath the waves.  Each day brought new perils – bandits lurking in shadowed forests, treacherous mountain passes, and the ever-present threat of starvation and disease.  But the allure of the unknown, the whispered tales of Ys and its untold riches, fueled his weary body and resolute spirit. The journey was as much a test of endurance as it was a search for glory.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the 'Stardust Drifter' through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her destination: Kepler-186f, a potentially habitable exoplanet light-years from Earth.  The vast emptiness of space, punctuated only by the distant shimmer of stars, was as daunting as any medieval wilderness.  Navigating asteroid fields, dodging space pirates, and maintaining the ship's fragile life support system were constant battles. But the promise of discovering new life, charting unknown galaxies, and expanding humanity's reach into the cosmos was a reward worth risking everything for.
