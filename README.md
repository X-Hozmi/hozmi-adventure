
Sir Reginald, his armor gleaming under the relentless sun, spurred his weary steed onward.  The trail, barely a track through the dense Ardennes forest, offered little respite from the sweltering heat.  Each creak of his saddle, each rustle in the undergrowth, fueled the gnawing fear of bandits or worse – the wild beasts rumored to haunt these woods. Yet, the lure of King Arthur's court and the promise of untold riches spurred him on, a knight errant on a perilous quest across the breadth of medieval England.

Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the 'Stardust Drifter' through the shimmering nebula of Xylos.  Her destination: Kepler-186f, a potential colony world light-years from Earth. Navigating asteroid fields and dodging rogue energy bursts was a daily challenge, but the vast, breathtaking beauty of the cosmos constantly reminded her of the incredible journey she was undertaking.  The thrill of discovery, the potential for life beyond Earth, far outweighed the risks of interstellar travel.
