
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the plains as Elara, her worn leather jerkin flapping, urged her mule onward.  The trail, little more than a rutted track, wound through a landscape of rolling hills and dark forests, the scent of woodsmoke and damp earth heavy in the air.  Her quest: to reach the fabled city of Zerzura, rumored to hold ancient secrets and untold riches.  Bandits, harsh weather, and the ever-present fear of the unknown were constant companions on her perilous journey across the medieval world, each sunrise a gamble against the odds.


Centuries later, Captain Jax navigated the swirling nebula of Xylos, the crimson light painting his starship's hull in a ghostly glow.  His mission: to locate the lost colony of Kepler-186f, scattered across the galaxy after a catastrophic warp storm.  Faster-than-light travel was commonplace, yet the vastness of space held its own terrors – unpredictable wormholes, hostile alien encounters, and the insidious decay of prolonged interstellar voyages.  Every jump through hyperspace was a leap of faith, a gamble against the infinite expanse of the cosmos.
