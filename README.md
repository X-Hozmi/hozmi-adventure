
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the rutted track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his mule onward.  Ahead lay the rumored city of Zerzura, a fabled oasis shimmering on the edge of the vast, unforgiving Sahara.  Bandits, starvation, and the relentless sun were constant companions on this perilous journey, yet the whispers of untold riches and ancient secrets spurred him on.  Each sunrise brought new challenges, from navigating treacherous mountain passes to bartering with suspicious desert tribes, all part of the thrilling, terrifying gamble he'd embraced.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the *Stardust Drifter* through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her destination: Kepler-186f, a potentially habitable exoplanet light years from Earth.  Navigating asteroid fields and wormhole instabilities was a daily occurrence, a far cry from the comparatively mundane dangers of medieval travel.  Yet the allure of discovery, the possibility of encountering alien life, and the sheer scale of the cosmos offered an adventure that dwarfed even the most daring medieval expedition; a voyage not just across lands, but across the very fabric of space and time.
