
The year is 1347.  A chill wind whipped across the rutted track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his mule onward.  He clutched the worn map, its parchment brittle with age, showing a route rumored to lead to the mythical city of Eldoria, a place whispered about in hushed tones in taverns and around crackling fires.  Bandit ambushes, treacherous mountain passes, and the ever-present threat of starvation were minor inconveniences compared to the lure of Eldoria's rumored riches and untold secrets. Each creak of the mule's saddle, each rustle in the undergrowth, fueled the adventure.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the starship 'Stardust Wanderer' through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her mission: to chart unexplored sectors of the Andromeda galaxy, searching for habitable planets and potential trade routes for the burgeoning intergalactic Federation.  The vast, inky blackness held both breathtaking beauty and terrifying unknowns – rogue asteroids, gravitational anomalies, and the ever-present possibility of encountering unknown, potentially hostile, alien civilizations.  Yet, the insatiable thirst for discovery, for pushing the boundaries of human exploration, burned brighter than any star.
