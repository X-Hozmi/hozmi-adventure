
The year is 1347.  Dust swirled behind Elara's mule as she crested the pass, the wind whipping her cloak around her like a frantic bird.  Ahead stretched the endless expanse of the Rhône valley, a tapestry woven with sun-baked fields and the distant glimmer of the Alps.  Her quest – to reach Avignon and deliver a vital message to the Pope – was fraught with peril.  Bandits lurked in the shadows of every forest, and the very air crackled with the whispers of plague. Yet, the thrill of the unknown, the challenge of the journey, fueled her onward, a tiny figure against the vastness of the medieval world.


Centuries later, Captain Rexx slammed the hyperspace controls, the stars blurring into streaks of incandescent colour.  His ship, the 'Stardust Drifter', hurtled through the void between galaxies, its destination: Xylos, a planet whispered to hold the key to interdimensional travel.  The journey was a gamble; hyperspace was unpredictable, and the Xylos system was notorious for its volatile nebulae.  But Rexx, seasoned spacefarer that he was, relished the danger, the cosmic uncertainty, the sheer impossibility of his mission. The galaxy, a canvas painted with nebulae and starlight, awaited.
