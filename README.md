
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the open plains as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his mount onward.  The journey from Canterbury to the Holy Land felt endless, a tapestry woven with the harsh beauty of sun-drenched vineyards, the chilling gloom of dense forests haunted by whispered tales of brigands, and the comforting camaraderie of fellow pilgrims sharing meager rations and stories under the vast, star-strewn sky.  Each sunrise brought a fresh wave of hope, a chance to overcome the perils of the road and inch closer to his sacred destination.

Millennia later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the *Starseeker* through the iridescent nebula of Xylos.  Her destination: Kepler-186f, a potentially habitable exoplanet light-years from Earth.  Navigating treacherous asteroid fields and dodging the unpredictable gravitational pulls of distant suns was a daily challenge, demanding unwavering precision and courage. But the allure of discovery, the thrill of charting unknown territories among breathtaking cosmic wonders, far outweighed the risks.  The vastness of space was both terrifying and exhilarating, a testament to the boundless human spirit's quest for the new.
