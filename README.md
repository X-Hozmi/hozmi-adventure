Whether driven by trade, holy pilgrimage, a monarch's decree, or the simple yearning for a better life, travelers faced a world where civilization was a thin veneer over vast, untamed wilderness. Roads were often mere tracks, prone to vanishing in mud or overgrown forest, making navigation a perilous art reliant on rudimentary maps, local gossip, and the sun, with every shadow potentially concealing brigands or wild beasts.

Every mile brought new challenges and potential wonders: the threat of outlaws lurking in dense woods, the biting wind on a desolate moor, or the sheer struggle of fording a swollen river.
