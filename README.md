
Sir Reginald, his chainmail gleaming under the harsh sun, spurred his destrier onward.  The dusty track stretched before him, a ribbon of ochre snaking through the unforgiving landscape.  He’d traded the comforts of his castle for the thrill of the unknown, a quest for the legendary Sunstone said to be hidden in the barbarian north.  Each creak of his saddle, each rustle in the tall grass, held the promise of danger – or discovery.  Bandits, wild beasts, and the treacherous terrain itself tested his mettle with every league traversed.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the *Stardust Drifter* through the iridescent nebula of Xylos.  Her destination: Kepler-186f, a planet rumored to hold the key to faster-than-light travel.  Navigating asteroid fields and dodging the gravitational pull of rogue planets was a daily challenge, a stark contrast to the carefully plotted jumps between star systems.  The vastness of space, both terrifying and exhilarating, dwarfed even the grandest medieval journeys, offering perils unlike any knight had ever faced.
