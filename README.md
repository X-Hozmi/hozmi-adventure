
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his mule onward.  The journey from York to the Holy Land felt endless, each day a tapestry woven with the dangers of bandits, treacherous terrain, and the ever-present threat of disease.  His meager supplies dwindled, replaced by the kindness of strangers and the grit of sheer survival.  The promise of Jerusalem, a destination whispered in hushed tones across taverns and campfires, fueled his weary steps; a pilgrimage of faith and adventure, fraught with peril and uncertainty.


Millennia later, Captain Elara Reyes piloted the *Stardust Drifter* through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  The jump to the Andromeda galaxy had been risky, a gamble on untested coordinates, but the potential reward – a legendary lost colony with advanced technology – was too tempting to ignore.  Navigating asteroid fields and dodging pirate vessels was a daily routine, the vast, indifferent expanse of space a constant reminder of her own fragility.  Yet, the thrill of discovery, the siren call of the unknown, propelled her forward across unimaginable distances, a modern-day explorer charting a course through the cosmic ocean.
