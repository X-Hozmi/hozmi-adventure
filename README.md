
In the medieval era, adventure across land was a perilous yet irresistible call into a world largely uncharted and rife with unknowns. Whether driven by the promise of trade riches, the sacred duty of pilgrimage, the pursuit of a mythic quest, or simply the urge to see what lay beyond the next forest, travelers embarked on journeys that demanded courage and resilience. The immediate challenges were formidable: rugged terrain, unpredictable weather, and the constant threat of bandits lurking on isolated roads, turning every mile into a test of survival against both nature and humanity.
