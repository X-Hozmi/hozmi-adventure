
The year is 1347.  Sir Reginald, his worn leather jerkin clinging to his frame, urged his weary mount onward.  The muddy track, barely a road, wound through a seemingly endless forest, the air thick with the smell of damp earth and decaying leaves.  His quest: to reach the mythical city of Eldoria, rumored to hold a cure for the plague ravaging his homeland.  Each creak of his saddle, each rustle in the undergrowth, fueled a mixture of hope and apprehension; bandits, wild beasts, and the very landscape itself posed constant threats on this perilous journey across the unforgiving terrain of medieval Europe.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the starship *Stardust*, its hyperdrive humming a song of unimaginable speed.  Ahead lay the Andromeda Galaxy, a swirling nebula of uncharted planets and untold possibilities.  Her mission: to locate a rare isotope crucial for sustaining life on a dying colony world.  Navigation through asteroid fields and encounters with unknown alien lifeforms were just a few of the hazards she faced; the vast emptiness of space was both a breathtaking wonder and a terrifying reminder of the loneliness and danger inherent in interstellar travel.
