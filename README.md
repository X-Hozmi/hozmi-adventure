
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the exposed plains as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his mount onward.  Ahead lay the treacherous Black Forest, whispered to be haunted by brigands and worse.  His quest, to deliver a vital message to the Duke of Burgundy, was fraught with peril; every rustle in the undergrowth, every shadow lengthening in the twilight, promised potential danger.  He clutched the sealed scroll, its wax seal a fragile promise of safety and reward, against the bitter cold.  Survival, he knew, depended not only on his skill with a sword but on his resilience against the elements and the unpredictable nature of the medieval world.


Centuries later, Captain Elara Reyes piloted the 'Stardust Wanderer' through the swirling nebula of Xylos, her breath catching in her throat as the ship shuddered against unforeseen gravitational currents.  Xylos was merely a stepping stone in her mission: to locate the lost colony of Kepler-186f, scattered across a galaxy far, far away.  Faster-than-light travel was commonplace, yet the vastness of space held its own terrors – rogue asteroids, hostile alien factions, and the ever-present threat of engine failure. The weight of her crew’s lives, and the hope of finding the lost colonists, rested on her shoulders as she navigated the uncharted expanse.
