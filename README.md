
The year is 1347.  Dust choked the air as Sir Reginald's retinue trudged across the sun-baked plains of Aquitaine.  Their journey, a pilgrimage to Santiago de Compostela, was fraught with peril. Bandits lurked in the shadows of ancient forests, disease stalked the crowded roads, and the unpredictable weather threatened to turn their arduous trek into a death march. Yet, each sunrise brought a renewed sense of purpose, a shared camaraderie forged in the crucible of hardship and the unwavering belief that their destination held the promise of salvation.  The endless horizon, a tapestry woven with the vibrant colors of freedom and uncertainty, beckoned them onward.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the starship 'Odyssey' through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her mission: to locate the lost colony of Kepler-186f, a planet rumored to hold the key to interstellar faster-than-light travel.  Navigating treacherous asteroid fields and dodging the unpredictable gravitational pull of rogue planets was a daily challenge.  But the vastness of space, a canvas painted with the cosmic wonders of nebulas and distant galaxies, fueled her determination.  The potential for discovery, the hope of finding a new home for humanity, pushed her and her crew towards the unknown depths of the cosmos.
