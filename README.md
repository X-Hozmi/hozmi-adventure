
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his mule onward.  The scent of woodsmoke and damp earth clung to him, a stark contrast to the exotic spices promised in the distant city of Baghdad.  Each creak of the cart, each rustle in the undergrowth, held the potential for banditry or worse – a fever that swept through travelers like a scythe.  Yet, the lure of the unknown, of silks and stories whispered in taverns, drove him ever closer to his perilous goal.  He yearned for the legendary tales to become his own reality.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova piloted the 'Stardust Drifter' through the swirling nebula of Xylos.  Her destination: Kepler-186f, a planet rumoured to hold the key to interstellar travel's next great leap.  The hum of the Alcubierre drive vibrated through her bones, a comforting counterpoint to the vast, silent expanse of space.  Navigation was a constant battle against gravitational anomalies and unpredictable asteroid fields, a far cry from the charted routes of Earth's solar system. But the promise of discovery, of unlocking secrets older than humanity itself, fueled her unwavering determination, even as the vast unknown stretched before her.
