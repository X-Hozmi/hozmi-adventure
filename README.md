
The year is 1347.  A biting wind whipped across the muddy track as Thomas, his worn leather jerkin flapping, urged his weary horse onward.  The scent of woodsmoke and damp earth filled his nostrils, a stark contrast to the tales whispered in taverns – of glittering silks in distant Samarkand, of fabled cities built of gold.  Each creak of his saddle, each flash of a hawk overhead, fueled his yearning for the unknown, a constant companion to the gnawing fear of bandits and disease lurking just beyond the next rise. His journey was a tapestry woven with hardship and hope, a gamble against the harsh realities of medieval travel.


Centuries later, Captain Elara Reyes gripped the controls of the *Stardust Drifter*, the vast nebula of Xylos painted across her viewport.  Her mission: to chart a course through the unmapped regions of the Andromeda galaxy, a journey measured not in days but in years.  The hum of the hyperdrive was a constant reminder of the incredible power harnessed – and the terrifying unknowns that lurked beyond the reach of known space.  Each jump through hyperspace, a leap of faith into the breathtaking and potentially deadly expanse of the cosmos, held the promise of discovery, a chance to add another star to the cartographers' charts.
