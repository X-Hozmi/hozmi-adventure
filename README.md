
The year is 1347.  Dust billows behind Elara's mule as she traverses the unforgiving landscape of the Pyrenees.  Each creak of the saddle, each strained breath of her weary steed, echoes the perilous journey ahead.  Bandit raids are a constant threat, whispers of the Black Death hang heavy in the air, and the treacherous mountain passes test both her endurance and her courage. Yet, the promise of reaching the fabled city of Samarkand, with its rumored silks and spices, fuels her onward, a beacon in the vast, unknown wilderness.  Every sunset paints a new challenge across the horizon.


Centuries later, Captain Eva Rostova pilots the starship *Stardust*, its sleek hull a testament to humanity's interstellar ambition.  The jump to Kepler-186f is imminent, a risky hyperspace leap across a volatile nebula.  Her crew, a diverse band of explorers, anxiously await the arrival at this potentially habitable planet, a world teeming with unknown possibilities, both exhilarating and terrifying. The vastness of space, a breathtaking panorama of nebulas and distant galaxies, is both a source of awe and a constant reminder of the inherent dangers of galactic travel.
